Tiramisu

En un bol se prepara la crema para el tiramisú: se mezcla el queso mascarpone con las yemas de huevo y el azúcar. Aparte, se baten las claras de huevo a punto de nieve. Finalmente, se incorporan las claras al bol del mascarpone. Se bate con cuidado, con las varillas, realizando movimientos envolventes, para no perder el aire de las claras montadas, (como haciendo una mousse).

In a bowl, prepare the cream for tiramisu: mix mascarpone cheese with egg yolks and sugar. Separately, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form. Finally, fold the egg whites into the mascarpone mixture. Carefully mix with a whisk, using folding motions, to preserve the airiness of the whipped whites (similar to making a mousse). In a deep tray, assemble the dessert by layering hard biscuits (ladyfingers) soaked in coffee. On a layer of coffee-soaked biscuits, add a layer of mascarpone cream, then another layer of biscuits, another layer of cream... and so on. Build as many layers as you like, finishing with a layer of cream. Dust a bit of bitter cocoa powder over the top layer. The tiramisu is a dessert that should be in the fridge for a few hours.
